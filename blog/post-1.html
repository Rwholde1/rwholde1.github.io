A Riftbound Set 3 Meta Guide

A friend lucky enough to get into the Riftbound Regional Qualifier in Vancouver, Canada asked me to write him a meta and sideboarding quide to help him prepare for the event. I had never written a guide like this before, but I gave it a shot to test my own understanding of the game and give my friend a primer for the decks he might see going into the event. It was a lot of fun to write and it forced me to nail down how I would classify each strategy and what I consider are the key cards to look out for in each deck. I'm excited to watch this qualifier and put my own knowledge to the test in events I'm playing in this weekend as well.

Irelia

This deck tends to follow a "protect the princess" strategy, where most of the cards played by the deck will protect or enhance a key unit. Irelia tends to be this key unit as it gains 1 might every time it is targeted with a spell or is readied. Stellacorn Herder can also take the role of the key unit due to it being an overwhelming card advantage engine, which when used with the maneuverability provided by Irelia and her kit elevates how oppressive this card can be. This deck attempts to maintain a consistent point tempo with cards that allow for more movement options and protect these key units with pump spells that allow them to hold battlefields and stay alive.

Key Cards

  • Counterspells
    • Defy
    • Not So Fast
  • Maneuverability
    • Boots of Swiftness
    • Ride the Wind
    • Flash
    • Irelia Legend
  • Survivability
    • Guardian Angel, Zhonya's Hourglass (3 - 1 split usually)
  • Pump Spells
    • Defiant Dance
    • Discipline
    • En Garde
  • Bounce
    • Gust
    • Star-Crossed
  • Adaptatron / Disarming Rake in Games 2/3

Sivir (Aurora)

This deck attempts to ramp into Dazzling Aurora as quickly as it can in order to play Elder Dragons (this will be true of many decks you face with access to orange). Sivir Aurora is the most consistent version of this strategy. The deck has access to Gust to mitigate early points, can take cards from opponent's hands with Sabotage and Mindsplitter, has access to consistency boosting cards including Lunar Boon, Stacked Deck, and Scryer's Bloom, and can utilize Last Rites so it never runs out of Units. Once Elder Dragon is in play, Flurry of Blades can be used to clear fields.

Key Cards

  • Ramp
    • Mobilize, Catalyst
    • Pack of Wonders + Treasure Trove
    • Sivir Legend Ability
  • Consistency
    • Lunar Boon
    • Stacked Deck
    • Scryer's Bloom
  • Dazzling Aurora
  • Elder Dragon, Mindsplitter (3-1 Split, 3-2 in games 2/3)
  • Gust
  • Sabotage
  • Recursion
    • Last Rites (Mainly)
    • Heedless Resurrection (Possible)
    • The Harrowing (Possible)
  • Abandon possibly played, more likely in games 2/3

Master Yi

This deck employs a midrange strategy and looks to outmuscle the opponent using the Master Yi passive. It sometimes utilizes the combo of Ruin Runner and Trinity Force for a strong untargetable unit that defends for 9 and holds for two points. Rengar serves as a 3/4 Deadbloom Predator that can take battlefields. Scuttle Crab allows the deck to look at the opponent's hand while maintaining 2 might at a battlefield on defense, generally deterring attacks during early turns as the opponent will generally need to use a card to win combat against it.

Key Cards

  • Ruin Runner
  • Trinity Force
  • Early Units
    • Scuttle Crab, Stalwart Poro, Lonely Poro, Clockwork Keeper (8-9)
  • Rengar (Orange)
  • MASTER YI PASSIVE (Do something to remind yourself of Yi Passive)
  • Pump Spells
    • Discipline
    • Punch First
    • En Garde
  • Alpha Strike
  • Defy
  • Hidden Cards
    • Zhonya's Hourglass
    • Back Off
  • Disarming Rake in the Sideboard
  • Vilemaw and Whiteflame Protector seen in some lists

Azir

Azir is a tempo/aggro deck that aims to play a gear each turn in order to activate the Azir Legend Ability. Eye of the Herald allows the generation of recruits, while Equipment like Brutalizer and B.F. Sword allow the Sand Soldiers to go tall. Once many Equipment Cards have been played, Arise can be used to generate a Sand Soldier for each as well as ready two, creating an entire board state from one card. Azir helps close out games by allowing all Token Units in play to move with him for a large attack.

Key Cards

  • Oops! All Gear
    • Eye of the Herald
    • Brutalizer
    • B.F. Sword
    • Guardian Angel (Mostly for Azir)
  • Defy
  • Guards!
  • Arise!
  • Discipline
  • Hidden Blade, Cull the Weak
  • Some decks have started to run 2x Yellow Vi in the main deck

Vex

This is a hold deck that will attempt to plant itself on one battlefield and hold there for the entire game. Generally the deck will play Overzealous Fan, Mutated Mouser, or Evelynn on turn one, then conquer turn two and play Vex - Apathetic to the battlefield and hide a card. This early game sets the deck up to hold out on that battlefield the rest of the game, playing Sona to have access to interaction, hiding cards like Back off, Evelynn, Teemo - Scout, or Switcheroo, and drawing cards with the Vex Legend Ability. This deck really wants to go first in order to establish point tempo: if the Vex deck misses a turn one play or is going second, the deck is significantly less strong. Watch out for the combination of Evelynn and Draven - Audacious (Draven attacks into a unit alone, Evelynn pulls the unit by being revealed from hidden, Draven wins the combat and scores a point).

Key Cards

  • Overzealous Fan
  • Mutated Mouser
  • Sona
  • Vex - Apathetic
  • Hidden Cards
    • Teemo - Scout
    • Evelynn
    • Switcheroo
    • Back off
    • Zhonya's Hourglass (Less popular, not much need to save units)
  • Interaction
    • Star Crossed
    • Existential Dread
    • Defy
    • Discipline
    • En Garde (If you see Irelia, they're probably playing this)
    • Charm (Usually less than 3 copies)
    • Vilemaw (Unlikely, very power-hungry deck)
  • Boots of Swiftness

LeBlanc

The hook version of this deck is the main one to watch out for. It is a tempo deck centered around Deathknell. The deck will try to have an early start, then ride an early point lead to the end of the game with the value generated by the deathknell units. The deck has a weak midgame, but the later the game goes, the stronger the deck is as it can develop its bigger units like Harnessed Dragon and Rift Herald. The deck can create battlefields with a lot of might from nothing using the LeBlanc Legend ability and Yellow Vi to ensure no units are lost during combat. The deck also uses Hidden Blade, Sacrifice, and Baited Hook to kill its own Units for their Deathknell effects, and gets them passively through Temporary on the Reflection Tokens.

Key Cards

  • Karthus
  • Baited Hook
  • Battlefields
    • Star Spring — Can conquer, make a Reflection, then play a unit to double conquer early
    • Windswept Hillock
    • Aspirant's Climb — Usually Going Second, cancels out with Arena's Greatest
  • Vi
  • Deathknell Units
    • Soaring Scout, Watchful Sentry, Black Rose Dignitary, LeBlanc Early
    • Ruined Rex, Glasc Mixologist Midgame
    • Rift Herald Late Game
  • Thousand-Tailed Watcher
  • Mirror Image
  • Will play Blue LeBlanc in Going First game
  • Limited Interaction — Only Vi, Hidden Blade, and Sacrifice

Diana

The deck is a midrange control deck that will attempt to outvalue opponents using Hwei's Move trigger. The deck plays a substantial interaction package — Star-Crossed, Existential Dread, Moonfall — it mulls through with Hwei in order to craft the perfect hand for any combat. The deck plays both Vex - Cheerless and Vex - Apathetic for holding battlefields, and can retake battlefields with Thousand-Tailed Watcher if necessary. The deck continually outvalues the opponent using Diana's Legend ability: in combat, the deck can play Stacked Deck or Gust with Diana Legend alone, giving it access to an extra energy every turn cycle.

Key Cards

  • Hwei (Just kill this and the deck ceases to function)
  • Diana
  • Holding
    • Vex - Apathetic
    • Vex - Cheerless
  • Interaction
    • Abandon / Hard Bargain (Usually one or the other)
    • Flash
    • Existential Dread
    • Moonfall
    • Star-Crossed
    • Ride the Wind (Conquer Free Battlefields First)
  • One Cost Action Cards
    • Stupefy
    • Stacked Deck
    • Gust
  • Thousand-Tailed Watcher (less copies usually)
  • Kha'Zix (sometimes played)
  • Fizz (sometimes played)
  • Turn to Dust / Fading Memories in Sideboard

It surprised me after writing this guide to find that pro players identified the same top 7 decks in the meta that I detailed in this article. It boosted my confidence in identifying the best decks in the format going forward. The current Riftbound meta is diverse and a lot of fun to play, and I'm excited to see the continued development of this game.
